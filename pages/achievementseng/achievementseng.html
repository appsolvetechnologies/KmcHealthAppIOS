ACHIEVEMENTS

Unprecedented activities and thumping achievements

  • Establishment of a Mosquito Research Laboratory.
  • Formation of 22 Rapid Action Teams to strengthen Vector Control activities.
  • 15 Dengue Detection Centres Commissioned, from which blood-test reports reach patients through SMS alert.
  • Free blood test-facilities for platelet count, malaria and dengue have been made available at all Ward Health Units of KMC.
  • Collection of dengue/malaria reports from different non-KMC health establishments by sending 144 persons on a daily basis.
  • Data Bank on Potential Mosquito Breeding Sources has been prepared.
  • Prevention of mosquito breeding by speedboat-generated waves demonstrated.
  • Larvicidal spray along canals is carried by using rowing boats as transport.
  • One staff in every ward and one staff at every borough oversee vector control activities as the Ward Vector Control Incharge and Borough Vector Control Incharge respectively.
  • Research activities have been published in:
    • A. Transactions of the Royal Society of Tropical Medicine and Hygiene 2013 (vol.107; pp. 147-151).
    • B. Dengue Bulletin of the World Health Organization 2011 (vol. 35, pp. 223-230).
    • C. Current Urban Studies 2014 [vol. 2, pp. 57-61]. D. Journal of Vector Borne Diseases 2011 [March issue].
  • Awareness-raising campaigns are mounted through screening of a documentary, distribution of multicoloured and multilingual leaflets, putting up of huge number of multicoloured banners/hoardings, health camps and awareness kiosks, workshops, FM radios, etc.
  • Malaria cases have declined from a dispiriting 96909 in 2010 to a comfortable 5564.
  • Dengue cases that otherwise would have remained unreported are now coming to surface.
